# Safety Regulations

## OSHA General Industry (29 CFR 1910)

### Subpart H - Hazardous Materials

#### 1910.119 - Process Safety Management (PSM)
```
Applies to:
- Processes with >10,000 lb of listed chemicals
- Oil and gas production generally exempt
- Refineries and gas plants covered

Key Elements:
1. Employee participation
2. Process safety information
3. Process hazard analysis
4. Operating procedures
5. Training
6. Contractors
7. Pre-startup safety review
8. Mechanical integrity
9. Hot work permits
10. Management of change
11. Incident investigation
12. Emergency planning
13. Compliance audits
14. Trade secrets
```

#### 1910.120 - HAZWOPER
```
Hazardous Waste Operations and Emergency Response

Training Levels:
- 24-hour: Occasional site workers
- 40-hour: Regular site workers
- 8-hour refresher: Annual requirement
- Emergency responders: 8-hour minimum

Applies to:
- Hazardous waste cleanups
- Emergency response to releases
- RCRA treatment/storage/disposal facilities
```

### Subpart I - Personal Protective Equipment

#### 1910.132 - General Requirements
```
Employer Responsibilities:
- Conduct hazard assessment
- Select appropriate PPE
- Provide PPE at no cost
- Train employees on use
- Ensure PPE is used
- Maintain PPE in serviceable condition
```

#### 1910.134 - Respiratory Protection
```
Written Program Required:
- Worksite-specific procedures
- Selection criteria
- Medical evaluations
- Fit testing (annual)
- Training
- Maintenance and care

Fit Testing:
- Initial before first use
- Annual thereafter
- When face/PPE changes
- Qualitative or quantitative
```

#### 1910.146 - Permit-Required Confined Spaces
```
Confined Space Characteristics:
- Large enough to enter
- Limited entry/exit
- Not designed for continuous occupancy

Permit-Required (one or more):
- Hazardous atmosphere
- Engulfment hazard
- Configuration hazard
- Other serious hazard

Program Requirements:
- Written program
- Hazard evaluation
- Entry permits
- Attendant requirements
- Rescue procedures
- Training
```

### Subpart J - General Environmental Controls

#### 1910.147 - Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)
```
Energy Control Program:
- Written procedures
- Employee training
- Periodic inspections

Lockout Steps:
1. Prepare for shutdown
2. Shut down equipment
3. Isolate energy sources
4. Apply lockout devices
5. Release stored energy
6. Verify isolation

Exceptions:
- Minor servicing during normal operations
- Cord and plug connected equipment
```

### Subpart Z - Toxic and Hazardous Substances

#### 1910.1200 - Hazard Communication (HazCom)
```
Program Requirements:
- Written program
- Container labeling
- Safety Data Sheets (SDS)
- Employee training

SDS Must Include:
1. Identification
2. Hazards
3. Composition
4. First aid
5. Fire-fighting
6. Accidental release
7. Handling and storage
8. Exposure controls/PPE
9. Physical/chemical properties
10. Stability/reactivity
11. Toxicological information
12. Ecological information
13. Disposal
14. Transport
15. Regulatory
16. Other
```

## OSHA Construction (29 CFR 1926)

### 1926.21 - Safety Training
```
Requirements:
- Hazard recognition training
- Safe work procedures
- Emergency procedures
- Equipment operation

Documentation:
- Training records
- Competent person designations
- Certifications
```

### 1926.500-503 - Fall Protection
```
Trigger Heights:
- General industry: 4 feet
- Construction: 6 feet
- Scaffolds: 10 feet

Protection Methods:
- Guardrail systems
- Safety net systems
- Personal fall arrest systems

Personal Fall Arrest:
- Full body harness
- Anchor point (5,000 lb capacity)
- Lanyard/SRL
- Proper connection
```

### 1926.550-556 - Cranes and Derricks
```
Requirements:
- Certified operators
- Annual inspections
- Load charts available
- Ground conditions verified
- Swing radius barricaded
```

## BSEE Safety and Environmental Management System (SEMS)

### 30 CFR 250.1900 - SEMS Requirements
```
13 SEMS Elements:

1. Safety and Environmental Policy
   - Management commitment
   - Resource allocation

2. Safety and Environmental Information
   - Hazard data
   - Equipment specifications
   - Procedures

3. Hazards Analysis
   - Process hazard analysis
   - Risk assessment
   - Recommendations

4. Management of Change (MOC)
   - Changes to equipment
   - Changes to procedures
   - Changes to organization

5. Operating Procedures
   - Written procedures
   - Safe work practices
   - Job safety analysis

6. Safe Work Practices
   - Permit systems
   - Lock out/tag out
   - Confined space entry

7. Training
   - Initial training
   - Refresher training
   - Documentation

8. Assurance of Quality (Mechanical Integrity)
   - Equipment design
   - Installation quality
   - Maintenance programs

9. Pre-Startup Review
   - New facilities
   - Modified facilities
   - Return to service

10. Emergency Response and Control
    - Emergency plans
    - Training
    - Drills

11. Investigation of Incidents
    - Root cause analysis
    - Corrective actions
    - Lessons learned

12. Audit of Safety and Environmental Management
    - Internal audits
    - Third-party audits
    - Corrective action tracking

13. Records and Documentation
    - Document control
    - Retention requirements
```

### Stop Work Authority (SWA)
```
SEMS Requirement:
- All personnel have authority
- No retaliation
- Written policy
- Training on use

When to Use:
- Imminent danger
- Deviation from procedures
- Changed conditions
- Equipment malfunction
- Uncertain of safe approach
```

## H2S Safety (29 CFR 1910.1000 / Industry Standards)

### Exposure Limits
```
OSHA PEL: 20 ppm (ceiling)
NIOSH REL: 10 ppm (10 min ceiling)
ACGIH TLV: 1 ppm (8-hour TWA), 5 ppm (STEL)

Industry Practice: 10 ppm action level

Immediately Dangerous (IDLH): 100 ppm
```

### H2S Contingency Plan
```
Required Contents:
1. Emergency contacts
2. Alarm systems and responses
3. Evacuation routes and muster points
4. Rescue procedures
5. Medical treatment
6. Equipment requirements
7. Training requirements
8. Notification procedures
```

### Safe Briefing Radius
```
Zone | Radius Calculation
-----|-------------------
100 ppm (IDLH) | Based on well potential
500 ppm | Public notification
Required personal monitors | <100 ppm radius

All personnel within radius must:
- Attend H2S safety briefing
- Have escape breathing apparatus
- Know alarm signals
- Know evacuation routes
```

## Well Control Regulations

### BSEE Well Control (30 CFR 250.400-490)
```
Requirements:
- Maintain well control
- Use proper equipment
- Train personnel
- Follow procedures

BOP Requirements:
- Configuration per CFR
- Pressure rated for conditions
- Tested per schedule
- Function tested weekly
- Pressure tested every 14 days
```

### IADC WellCAP/IWCF
```
Industry Certification:
- WellCAP (IADC)
- IWCF (International)

Levels:
- Awareness
- Driller
- Supervisor
- Engineer

Validity: 2 years (typical)
```

```
Training Records Must Include:
- Employee name
- Training date
- Topic covered
- Trainer name/qualification

- Certification (if applicable)

Retain: Duration of employment + 3 years
```

## Safety Inspection Requirements

### Daily Inspections
```
- BOP visual inspection
- Drilling fluid properties
- Safety equipment check
- PPE condition
- Work area housekeeping
- Fire extinguisher check
```

### Weekly Inspections
```
- BOP function test
- Accumulator test
- Safety meeting documentation
- First aid equipment
- Emergency equipment
```

### Periodic Inspections
```
- BOP pressure test (14 days)
- Crane inspection (annual)
- Lifting equipment (quarterly)
- Pressure vessels (annual)
- Fire suppression systems (annual)
- Emergency response drills (varies)
```
