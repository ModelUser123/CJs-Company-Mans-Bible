# Federal Regulations (BSEE/BLM)

## Bureau of Safety and Environmental Enforcement (BSEE)

### Jurisdiction
- Outer Continental Shelf (OCS)
- Federal waters beyond state jurisdiction
- Typically 3 miles from shore (9 miles in Texas/Florida Gulf)

### 30 CFR Part 250 - Key Sections

#### Subpart A - General (250.100-199)
```
250.107 - What must I do to protect health, safety, property, and the environment?
- Conduct all activities safely
- Take immediate action during emergencies
- Report unsafe conditions
- Comply with all lease terms

250.130 - What are my recordkeeping requirements?
- Retain for 3-6 years depending on record type
- Make available for BSEE inspection
- Electronic records acceptable with proper backup
```

#### Subpart D - Oil and Gas Drilling Operations (250.400-490)
```
250.401 - What are the requirements for drilling operations?
- APD approval required before spudding
- Follow approved drilling prognosis
- Maintain well control equipment
- Qualified personnel required

250.410 - What drilling fluid/cuttings requirements apply?
- Maintain proper mud weight
- Monitor drilling fluid properties
- Proper disposal of cuttings
- Follow EPA discharge requirements

250.414-420 - Casing and cementing requirements
- Conductor: Set and cemented
- Surface: Protect freshwater zones
- Intermediate: As required by conditions
- Production: Isolate producing zones
- All cement jobs must reach shoe with returns

250.440-445 - Well control requirements
- Maintain sufficient hydrostatic pressure
- Monitor for kicks
- Shut in per approved procedures
- Report all well control events
```

#### Subpart E - Oil and Gas Well-Completion Operations (250.500-517)
```
250.500 - What are the requirements for completion operations?
- Submit completion/workover application
- Install proper safety equipment
- Conduct all work safely
- Proper testing before production

250.514-515 - Tubing and packer requirements
- Tubing opposite all perforations
- Packer set within 200 ft above perforations
- Tubing must withstand expected pressures
```

#### Subpart F - Oil and Gas Well-Workover Operations (250.600-618)
```
250.601 - General requirements
- Workover approval required
- Well control equipment in place
- Competent personnel on site

250.615-621 - BOP requirements for workovers
- BOP stack requirements based on H2S
- Testing requirements: Weekly
- Function testing required
```

#### Subpart G - Well Operations and Equipment (250.700-750)
```
250.720-739 - BOP equipment requirements
- Annular preventer required
- Blind and shear rams required
- Pipe rams for all pipe sizes in hole
- Dual pod control system
- Accumulator capacity requirements

250.731 - BOP testing requirements
- Pressure test: Before use, every 14 days, after repair
- Function test: Every 7 days
- Test to rated working pressure (or 70% if higher than MAASP)

250.737-740 - Drill-through equipment
- Annular BOP
- Inner seal
- Proper certification required
```

#### Subpart S - SEMS (250.1700-1760)
```
250.1700 - Safety and Environmental Management Systems
- Applies to all OCS operations
- Must have documented SEMS program
- Annual audits required
- Management of change procedures
- Stop Work Authority required

Key SEMS Elements:
1. General (management commitment)
2. Safety and Environmental Information
3. Hazards Analysis
4. Management of Change
5. Operating Procedures
6. Safe Work Practices
7. Training
8. Mechanical Integrity
9. Pre-startup Review
10. Emergency Response
11. Investigation of Incidents
12. Audit of Safety and Environmental Management
13. Records and Documentation
```

|-------|-------------------|

## Bureau of Land Management (BLM)

### Jurisdiction
- Federal and Indian lands
- Split estate situations
- National forests (coordination with USFS)

### 43 CFR 3160 - Onshore Oil and Gas Operations

#### Subpart 3162 - Requirements for Operating Rights Owners and Operators
```
3162.1 - General requirements
- Diligent development
- Prevention of waste
- Protection of mineral resources
- Compliance with lease terms

3162.5 - Environmental requirements
- Surface use plan required
- Cultural resource survey
- Threatened/endangered species review
- Surface reclamation requirements
```

### BLM Onshore Orders

#### Onshore Order #1 - Approval of Operations
```
- APD required before drilling
- Surface use plan included
- Drilling plan with casing/cementing program
- Cultural resources clearance
- Environmental review (NEPA)
- BIA approval for Indian lands
```

#### Onshore Order #2 - Drilling Operations
```
Section 1: Drilling prognosis requirements
- Formation tops expected
- Casing points
- Mud program
- Hole problems anticipated

Section 2: Casing requirements
- Conductor: Set below fresh water
- Surface: 10% of TD or specific depth
- Intermediate: As required
- Production: To isolate producing zones

Section 3: Cementing requirements
- Cement to surface on all casing (or approved alternative)
- CBL may be required
- Remedial cementing if inadequate

Section 4: BOP requirements
- Must have working BOP
- Testing per BSEE-equivalent standards
- H2S equipment if zone is SOUR

Section 5: Drilling operations
- Notify 24 hours before spud
- Formation tops reported
- Mud weights maintained
- Deviation surveys required
```

#### Onshore Order #3 - Site Security
```
- Access control measures
- Theft prevention
- Seal requirements for measurement
- Production accountability
```

#### Onshore Order #4 - Measurement of Oil
```
- LACT unit requirements
- Tank gauging procedures
- Meter proving
- Production reporting
```

#### Onshore Order #5 - Measurement of Gas
```
- Meter specifications
- Calibration requirements
- Gas analysis
- Heating value determination
```

#### Onshore Order #7 - Disposal of Produced Water
```
- Injection wells
- Commercial disposal
- Surface disposal limitations
- Pit requirements
```

### APD Process (BLM)

```
Step 1: Pre-Application Meeting (recommended)
        ↓
Step 2: Submit APD Package
        - Form 3160-3
        - Surface Use Plan
        - Drilling Plan
        - Cultural Survey
        - Wildlife Survey
        ↓
Step 3: BLM Review (30-60 days target)
        - Completeness check
        - Environmental review
        - Technical review
        ↓
Step 4: NEPA Decision
        - Categorical Exclusion (CE)
        - Environmental Assessment (EA)
        - Environmental Impact Statement (EIS)
        ↓
Step 5: Conditions of Approval (COA)
        ↓
Step 6: APD Approved
        ↓
Step 7: Post-Approval Requirements
        - Bond verified
        - 24-hour spud notice
```

### BLM Reporting Requirements

| Report | When Due |
|--------|----------|
| Spud notice | 24 hours before |
| Drilling report | Weekly while drilling |
| Completion report | 30 days after completion |
| Sundry notice | Before any major operation |
| Well logs | 30 days after completion |
| Production report | Monthly |

## EPA Regulations Affecting Drilling

### Clean Water Act
- NPDES permits for discharge
- Stormwater management
- Wetlands protection (Section 404)
- Spill prevention (SPCC)

### RCRA (Waste Management)
- Exempt vs. non-exempt wastes
- Used oil management
- Hazardous waste identification
- Manifest requirements

### Clean Air Act
- NSPS for oil and gas
- Methane emission rules
- VOC limits
- Flaring restrictions

### NEPA Requirements
- Environmental review for federal actions
- CE, EA, or EIS determination
- Public comment periods
- Cumulative impact analysis

---

*Federal regulations are minimum standards. State regulations may be more stringent and must also be followed.*
