# State Regulations Overview

## Texas - Railroad Commission (RRC)

### Statewide Rules
```
Rule 8: Water Protection
- Protects usable-quality water
- Casing requirements
- Pit and disposal requirements

Rule 13: Casing and Cementing
- Surface casing: Protect fresh water (200 ft below deepest fresh water)
- Cement to surface required
- Production casing requirements
- Cement bond logs may be required

Rule 14: Plugging
- Plug requirements for abandonment
- Cement plug specifications
- Surface restoration

Rule 36: Oil Well Spacing
- Standard: 467 ft from lease line, 1200 ft between wells
- Density based on reservoir

Rule 37: Well Spacing Exceptions
- Procedure for requesting variance
- Evidence requirements

Rule 38: Gas Well Spacing
- Varies by field
- Typically 467-1320 ft from lease line

Rule 79: Surface Casing Depth
- Based on groundwater protection
- District-specific requirements
```

### Permitting (Texas)
```
W-1: Drilling Permit
- $200 base fee + bond
- 5-day processing for standard
- Expedited available

W-1A: Oil Well Completion/Recompletion
W-2: Gas Well Completion
G-1: Gas Well Deliverability Test
P-4: Monthly Production Report (due 45 days after month end)
```

## Oklahoma - Corporation Commission (OCC)

### Key Regulations
```
OAC 165:10 - Oil and Gas Conservation
- Well spacing orders
- Increased density applications
- Drilling units

Surface Casing:
- 50 ft below base of freshwater
- Cement to surface
- Wait on cement: 8 hours minimum

Intermediate/Production Casing:
- Per approved permit
- Cement 500 ft above producing zone (min)
```

### Permitting (Oklahoma)
```
Intent to Drill: Form 1000
- File before spud
- $250+ fees
- Spacing verification required

Completion Report: Form 1002B
- Due within 30 days

Monthly Production: Form 1012/1012A
- Due by 15th of following month
```

## New Mexico - Oil Conservation Division (OCD)

### Key Regulations
```
NMAC 19.15 - Oil and Gas
- Casing program approval required
- Surface casing to protect all fresh water
- Cement to surface or 200 ft above shoe

Spacing:
- 330 ft from property line (standard)
- Field rules may differ
- Exceptions possible
```

### Permitting (New Mexico)
```
C-101: Application for Permit to Drill
- Submit 10 days before spud
- Bond required

C-103: Subsequent Report
- Due within 15 days of completion

C-115: Monthly Production Report
- Due by 15th of second following month
```

## Louisiana - Dept. of Natural Resources (LDNR)

### Key Regulations
```
LAC 43:XIX - Statewide Order 29-B
- Drilling and production operations
- Well control requirements
- Environmental protection

Casing Requirements:
- Protection of fresh water required
- Cement to surface on surface casing
- BOP testing every 14 days

Coastal Zone:
- Additional permits required
- Coastal Use Permit (CUP)
- More stringent requirements
```

### Permitting (Louisiana)
```
Work Permit:
- Required before any drilling
- Bond required
- Cultural resource clearance

Completion Report:
- Due within 30 days

Monthly Production: OMR-04
- Due by 15th of following month
```

## North Dakota - Industrial Commission (NDIC)

### Key Regulations
```
NDAC 43-02 - Oil and Gas
- Well spacing: 1280 acre units (2 wells per section)
- Surface casing: 50 ft below fresh water
- Cement circulation required
- Pressure testing of casing

Bakken-Specific:
- Horizontal well spacing rules
- Multi-well pad development
- Increased density applications
```

### Permitting (North Dakota)
```
Application to Drill:
- Form 1 and sundry bond
- 7-10 day approval (routine)

Completion Report: Form 6
- Due within 30 days

Monthly Production Report:
- Due by 20th of following month
```

## Colorado - COGCC/ECMC

### Recent Changes (SB 19-181)
```
- Mission changed from "fostering" to "regulating"
- Local government authority enhanced
- Setback requirements increased
- Cumulative impact considerations
```

### Key Regulations
```
Rule 301-330: Well Construction
- Surface casing into competent zone
- Cement to surface
- Intermediate casing for USDW protection

Rule 900 Series: Setbacks
- 2000 ft from occupied buildings (default)
- 2640 ft from schools/hospitals
- Local governments may increase

Rule 1000: Hydraulic Fracturing
- Chemical disclosure
- Baseline water sampling
- Flowback management
```

### Permitting (Colorado)
```
Form 2: Application for Permit to Drill
- Comprehensive application
- Location assessment review
- Local government consultation

Form 5A: Completion/Recompletion
- Due within 30 days

Form 7: Monthly Production
- Due by last day of following month
```

## Pennsylvania - DEP

### Key Regulations (Unconventional)
```
Chapter 78a: Unconventional Wells
- Surface casing: 50 ft into competent rock below fresh water
- Cement to surface required
- Intermediate casing may be required
- Pit restrictions/containment
```

### Permitting (Pennsylvania)
```
Well Permit:
- Form 5500-PM-OG0001
- Plat and bond required
- 45-day review period

Completion Report:
- Due within 30 days

Production Reports:
- Annual production (due February 15)
- Waste reports
```
