## Coiled Tubing Overview

### What is Coiled Tubing?

```
COILED TUBING BASICS
├── Definition
│   ├── Continuous steel tubing
│   ├── Spooled on large reel
│   ├── Injected into well
│   └── No connections while running
├── Typical Sizes
│   ├── 1" to 3-1/2" OD
│   ├── Most common: 1-1/2" to 2-3/8"
│   ├── Wall thickness: 0.087" to 0.204"
│   └── Length: 10,000-30,000+ ft
├── Key Advantages
│   ├── Continuous string (no connections)
│   ├── Fast trip speeds
│   ├── Can work under pressure
│   ├── Smaller footprint
│   └── Rigless operations
└── Limitations
    ├── No rotation capability
    ├── Fatigue life concerns
    ├── Limited weight capacity
    ├── Cannot make up connections
    └── Buckling in compression
```

## Safety Considerations

### CT-Specific Hazards

| Hazard | Risk | Control |
|--------|------|---------|
| Tubing failure | Release, injury | Fatigue tracking, inspection |
| High pressure | Injection, injury | Pressure ratings, PPE |
| Pinch points | Crushing | Guards, procedures |
| Stored energy | Sudden release | Proper procedures |
| H2S exposure | Toxic | Monitoring, SCBA |
