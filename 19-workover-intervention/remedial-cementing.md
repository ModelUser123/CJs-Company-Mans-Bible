## Squeeze Cementing

### Squeeze Cement Fundamentals

```
SQUEEZE CEMENT PROCESS
├── Definition
│   ├── Force cement into formation
│   ├── Through perforations or casing damage
│   ├── Fill voids behind casing
│   └── Create seal/barrier
├── Objectives
│   ├── Seal perforations
│   ├── Fill channels
│   ├── Repair casing
│   └── Isolate zones
├── Squeeze Pressure
│   ├── Must exceed pore pressure
│   ├── Should not exceed frac pressure
│   ├── Typically 0.5-1.0 psi/ft overbalance
│   └── Monitor carefully
└── Success Indicators
    ├── Pressure builds as designed
    ├── No leakoff after set
    ├── Pressure test passes
    └── Production test confirms
```

### Types of Squeeze Jobs

| Type | Description | Application |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Low pressure | Below frac gradient | Sealing perfs, channels |
| High pressure | At or above frac | Matrix squeeze, deep penetration |
| Hesitation | Intermittent pumping | Reduce filtrate loss |
| Block squeeze | Large volume | Fill large voids |
| Running squeeze | Continuous pump | Standard method |

### Squeeze Cementing Procedure

```
SQUEEZE CEMENT PROCEDURE
├── Preparation
│   ├── Kill well if needed
│   ├── Condition wellbore
│   ├── Perforate/prepare squeeze zone
│   ├── Design cement slurry
│   └── Calculate volumes
├── Run Tools (Packer Method)
│   ├── Run squeeze packer
│   ├── Spot packer above zone
│   ├── Set packer
│   ├── Test packer
│   └── Establish injectivity
├── Pump Cement
│   ├── Pump spacer (if needed)
│   ├── Displace cement to zone
│   ├── Apply squeeze pressure
│   ├── Monitor pressure response
│   └── Hold at final pressure
├── Displace/Reverse
│   ├── Reverse out excess cement
│   ├── Or bullhead to contamination point
│   ├── Clean above packer
│   └── Release packer
├── WOC
│   ├── Wait on cement (8-24 hours)
│   ├── Time based on slurry design
│   └── Temperature dependent
├── Test
│   ├── Pressure test (injectivity)
│   ├── Production test (isolation)
│   └── Verify objectives met
└── If Unsuccessful
    ├── Re-squeeze
    ├── Drill out and repeat
    └── Alternative method
```

### Squeeze Without Packer (Bradenhead)

```
BRADENHEAD SQUEEZE
├── When Used
│   ├── Cannot set packer
│   ├── Large area to squeeze
│   ├── Shallow zones
│   └── Emergency situations
├── Procedure
│   ├── Position tubing above zone
│   ├── Close annular preventer/packer
│   ├── Pump cement down tubing
│   ├── Displace to zone
│   ├── Apply pressure (against closed annular)
│   └── Hold at squeeze pressure
├── Advantages
│   ├── Simple equipment
│   ├── Can squeeze multiple zones
│   └── Good for channels
├── Disadvantages
│   ├── Less precise placement
│   ├── May cement tubing
│   └── Requires annular integrity
└── Note: Not preferred for precise work
```

## Cement Plug Setting

### Balanced Plug Method

```
BALANCED PLUG PROCEDURE
├── Concept
│   ├── Cement column equal inside/outside
│   ├── When pipes pulled, plug remains
│   ├── Critical depth calculation
│   └── Most common method
├── Calculations
│   ├── Cement volume needed
│   ├── Spacer volumes (above/below)
│   ├── Displacement volume
│   ├── Height of plug
│   └── Balance point
├── Procedure
│   ├── Run open-ended tubing/DP
│   ├── Spot at plug depth
│   ├── Pump spacer ahead
│   ├── Pump cement
│   ├── Pump spacer behind
│   ├── Displace exactly (critical!)
│   ├── Pull pipe slowly
│   ├── WOC
│   └── Tag plug
└── Key Success Factors
    ├── Accurate displacement
    ├── Proper spacers
    ├── Slow pipe movement
    └── Correct slurry design
```

### Dump Bailer Method

```
DUMP BAILER PLUG
├── When Used
│   ├── Precise placement needed
│   ├── Small volume
│   ├── Slickline operation
│   └── Top of plug critical
├── Equipment
│   ├── Dump bailer tool
│   ├── Wireline/slickline
│   ├── Premixed cement
│   └── Release mechanism
├── Procedure
│   ├── Fill bailer with cement
│   ├── Run to depth
│   ├── Release cement
│   ├── Multiple runs if needed
│   └── WOC and tag
├── Advantages
│   ├── Very precise
│   ├── No displacement issues
│   ├── Small volumes
│   └── Rigless possible
└── Disadvantages
    ├── Limited volume per run
    ├── Multiple trips
    ├── Time consuming
    └── Slurry stability critical
```

### Coiled Tubing Cement Plug

```
CT CEMENT PLUG
├── Advantages
│   ├── Continuous pipe in hole
│   ├── Precise placement
│   ├── Can work under pressure
│   └── Faster than jointed pipe
├── Procedure

├── Considerations

│   ├── Calculate pullback rate
│   └── Avoid trapping CT

    ├── Sidetrack plugs
    ├── Zone isolation
    └── Lost circulation
```

## Cement Slurry Design

### Remedial Cement Properties

```
REMEDIAL SLURRY REQUIREMENTS
├── Fluid Loss Control
│   ├── Critical for squeezes
│   ├── Target: <50 ml/30 min
│   ├── Reduces dehydration
│   └── Better penetration
├── Thickening Time
│   ├── Adequate for placement
│   ├── Plus safety margin
│   ├── Temperature dependent
│   └── Typically 2-4 hours
├── Compressive Strength
│   ├── Develop quickly
│   ├── 500 psi minimum
│   ├── 1000+ psi preferred
│   └── Temperature dependent
├── Free Water
│   ├── Zero or near-zero
│   ├── Prevents channels
│   └── Critical for plugs
└── Rheology
    ├── Pumpable
    ├── Not too thick
    ├── Stable
    └── Compatible with spacers
```

### Common Remedial Cement Systems

| System | Application | Key Properties |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Neat + fluid loss | Standard squeeze | Low fluid loss |
| Microfine | Tight formations | Very fine particle |
| Latex modified | Flexible seal | Good bonding |
| Expanding | Fill voids | Expands on set |
| Resin | Small volumes, hot | Chemical set |

### Cement Additives for Remedial Work

| Additive | Purpose | Typical Concentration |
|----------|---------|----------------------|
| Fluid loss additive | Reduce dehydration | 0.3-1.0% BWOC |
| Dispersant | Reduce viscosity | 0.1-0.5% BWOC |
| Retarder | Extend pump time | As needed |
| Accelerator | Speed set | As needed |
| Latex | Flexibility, bond | 1-3 gal/sk |
| Expansion additive | Fill voids | 0.5-1.5% BWOC |

### When to Repeat

| Test Result | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| Fails pressure test | Re-squeeze or drill out |
| Partial success | May re-squeeze |
| Complete failure | Re-evaluate, different method |
| Success | Proceed with operations |
