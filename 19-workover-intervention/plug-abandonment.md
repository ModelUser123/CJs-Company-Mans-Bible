# Plug and Abandonment (P&A)

## Overview

### Why P&A is Required

```
P&A TRIGGERS
├── Economic
│   ├── Well uneconomic to operate
│   ├── Repair costs exceed value
│   └── No viable recompletion options
├── Technical
│   ├── Reservoir depleted
│   ├── Irreparable mechanical failure
│   ├── Wellbore integrity compromised
│   └── Safety concerns
├── Regulatory
│   ├── Lease expiration
│   ├── Regulatory order
│   ├── Environmental requirement
│   └── Orphan well program
└── Strategic
    ├── Field abandonment
    ├── Asset sale requirement
    └── Liability reduction
```

### Regulatory Requirements

| Jurisdiction | Key Requirements |
|--------------|-----------------|
| BSEE (Offshore) | 30 CFR 250, platform removal |
| BLM (Federal onshore) | 43 CFR 3162 |
| State (varies) | State oil/gas commission rules |
| General | Isolate all zones, protect USDW |

## Cement Plug Requirements

### Plug Placement Requirements

```
TYPICAL PLUG LOCATIONS
├── Bottom Plug
│   ├── Below all perforations
│   ├── In open hole below casing
│   ├── Minimum 100 ft length
│   └── Tagged and tested
├── Zone Isolation Plugs
│   ├── Above each productive zone
│   ├── Across perforations
│   ├── Minimum 50-100 ft above perfs
│   └── Must isolate zone
├── Casing Stub Plugs
│   ├── At each casing shoe
│   ├── At liner tops
│   ├── 50-100 ft minimum
│   └── Extend above and below shoe
├── Surface Plug
│   ├── Top of well
│   ├── Extend into surface casing
│   ├── Minimum 50-100 ft
│   └── Protects USDW
└── Special Plugs
    ├── At any abnormal pressure zones
    ├── H2S zones
    ├── Lost circulation zones
    └── Problem areas
```

### Cement Requirements

| Plug Type | Minimum Length | Verification |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| Bottom plug | 100 ft | Tag, test if possible |
| Zone isolation | 50-100 ft | Tag, may test |
| Casing shoe | 50-100 ft | Tag |
| Surface | 50-100 ft | Visual, marker required |

### Cement Specifications

```
P&A CEMENT REQUIREMENTS
├── Slurry Design
│   ├── API Class A, C, G, or H
│   ├── Appropriate for depth/temp
│   ├── Zero free water
│   ├── Good compressive strength
│   └── No gas migration additives
├── Compressive Strength
│   ├── Minimum 500 psi (24 hr)
│   ├── Typically 1000+ psi preferred
│   └── Temperature dependent
├── Thickening Time
│   ├── Adequate for placement
│   ├── Safety factor included
│   └── Account for conditions
└── Volume
    ├── Calculate displacement carefully
    ├── Excess for contamination
    └── Account for hole conditions
```

## P&A Procedure

### Preparation

```
PRE-P&A PREPARATION
├── Permits
│   ├── File intent with state/BSEE
│   ├── Obtain approval
│   ├── Submit P&A plan
│   └── Schedule inspection if required
├── Equipment
│   ├── Workover rig or CT
│   ├── Cement unit
│   ├── Wireline (if needed)
│   ├── Well control equipment
│   └── Surface restoration equipment
├── Well Preparation
│   ├── Kill well if producing
│   ├── Remove wellhead equipment
│   ├── Pull tubing/completion
│   ├── Clean out fill/debris
│   └── Assess casing condition
└── Materials
    ├── Cement
    ├── Additives
    ├── Spacers
    └── Bridge plugs (if used)
```

### Step-by-Step Procedure

```
P&A EXECUTION STEPS
├── 1. Kill Well
│   ├── Ensure well is dead
│   ├── Kill weight fluid
│   └── Verify no flow
├── 2. Remove Completion
│   ├── Pull tubing
│   ├── Pull packer
│   ├── Recover all equipment
│   └── Clean out wellbore
├── 3. Assess Wellbore
│   ├── Run CBL if needed
│   ├── Evaluate casing condition
│   ├── Identify problem areas
│   └── Adjust plan if needed
├── 4. Set Bottom Plug
│   ├── Clean out to TD
│   ├── Set bridge plug (optional)
│   ├── Pump cement
│   ├── WOC
│   └── Tag plug
├── 5. Set Zone Plugs
│   ├── For each productive zone
│   ├── Squeeze perforations first (if required)
│   ├── Set cement plug above zone
│   ├── WOC
│   └── Tag and verify
├── 6. Set Shoe Plugs
│   ├── At each casing shoe
│   ├── Minimum required length
│   ├── WOC
│   └── Tag
├── 7. Set Surface Plug
│   ├── Cut casing below surface
│   ├── Set cement to surface
│   ├── Install marker (required)
│   └── May weld cap on casing
├── 8. Surface Restoration
│   ├── Remove all equipment
│   ├── Cut casing 3-6 ft below surface
│   ├── Backfill
│   └── Restore surface
└── 9. Documentation
    ├── Complete P&A report
    ├── Submit to regulatory
    ├── Update records
    └── Release bond
```

### Bridge Plug vs Cement Plug

| Method | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Bridge plug + cement | Positive foundation | Cost, debris |
| Balanced cement plug | Simpler, cheaper | May slip, contaminate |
| Squeeze + cement | Best seal | More operations |

## Special P&A Situations

### Multiple Zone P&A

```
MULTI-ZONE P&A
├── Order of Operations
│   ├── Usually bottom to top
│   ├── Isolate each zone
│   ├── Verify each plug
│   └── No shortcuts
├── Squeeze May Be Required
│   ├── If cement behind casing poor
│   ├── If zonal isolation questionable
│   └── Regulatory may require
└── Documentation Critical
    ├── Each zone must be verified
    ├── Tests documented
    └── Report all plugs
```

### Casing Failure/Poor Cement

```
IMPAIRED WELLBORE P&A
├── If Casing Damaged
│   ├── Section mill and remove
│   ├── Underream and cement
│   ├── Establish formation seal
│   └── More expensive
├── If Poor Cement Behind Casing
│   ├── Perforate and squeeze
│   ├── Cut casing and cement
│   ├── Establish barrier
│   └── May need multiple attempts
└── If Cannot Access TD
    ├── Set plugs as deep as possible
    ├── Document limitations
    ├── May require waiver
    └── Additional surface protection
```

### Offshore P&A

```
OFFSHORE SPECIAL REQUIREMENTS
```

## Testing and Verification

### Plug Verification

```
CEMENT PLUG VERIFICATION
├── Tagging
│   ├── Tag top of cement
│   ├── Verify at calculated depth
│   ├── Apply weight (5,000-10,000 lb)
│   └── Record actual depth
├── Pressure Testing
│   ├── Apply pressure above plug
│   ├── Typically 1,000-1,500 psi
│   ├── Hold 15-30 minutes
│   ├── No pressure loss = success
│   └── Not always required
├── Inflow Testing
│   ├── Reduce pressure below
│   ├── Monitor for flow
│   ├── No flow = effective plug
│   └── Offshore requirement
└── Documentation
    ├── Record all tests
    ├── Signed by witness (if required)
    └── Include in final report
```

### Plug Test Requirements

| Test Type | When Required | Acceptance |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Weight test (tag) | All plugs | ±10 ft of calculated |
| Pressure test | Regulatory requirement | Zero leakage |
| Inflow test | Offshore, some states | No flow |
| Witness | Regulatory requirement | Inspector present |
