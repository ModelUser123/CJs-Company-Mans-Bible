# Recompletion Operations

## Recompletion Overview

### When to Recomplete

```
RECOMPLETION TRIGGERS
├── Zone Change Needed
│   ├── Current zone depleted
│   ├── Water/gas breakthrough
│   ├── Better zone identified
│   └── Regulatory requirement
├── Production Enhancement
│   ├── Add perforations
│   ├── Restimulation needed
│   ├── Change completion type
│   └── Optimize production
├── Mechanical Issues
│   ├── Current completion damaged
│   ├── Need different lift method
│   ├── Sand control required
│   └── Equipment upgrade
└── Economic Optimization
    ├── Behind-pipe reserves
    ├── Better zone economics
    ├── Commingling opportunities
    └── Field development plan
```

### Recompletion Types

| Type | Description | Typical Duration |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Add perfs | Perforate additional interval | 1-3 days |
| Zone change | Squeeze old, perf new | 3-7 days |
| Restimulation | Frac/acidize existing zone | 2-5 days |
| Lift change | Change artificial lift method | 3-7 days |
| Dual completion | Add second zone/string | 5-10 days |

## Zone Change Recompletion

### Planning for Zone Change

```
ZONE CHANGE PLANNING
├── Target Zone Evaluation
│   ├── Log analysis
│   ├── Offset well data
│   ├── Formation pressure
│   ├── Expected production
│   └── Completion requirements
├── Current Zone Disposition
│   ├── Squeeze and abandon
│   ├── Leave producing (commingled)
│   ├── Mechanical isolation
│   └── Regulatory requirements
├── Wellbore Considerations
│   ├── Casing condition at new zone
│   ├── Cement behind casing
│   ├── Any restrictions
│   └── Access to zone
└── Completion Design
    ├── Open hole vs cased
    ├── Perforation design
    ├── Stimulation needs
    ├── Artificial lift
    └── Monitoring equipment
```

### Zone Change Procedure

```
ZONE CHANGE STEPS
├── 1. Kill Well
│   ├── Bullhead or circulate
│   ├── Appropriate kill weight
│   └── Verify dead
├── 2. Pull Completion
│   ├── Remove tubing
│   ├── Release/retrieve packer
│   ├── Remove any restrictions
│   └── Recover all equipment
├── 3. Isolate Old Zone
│   ├── Run CBL if needed
│   ├── Squeeze perforations
│   ├── Set cement plug
│   ├── Verify isolation
│   └── Pressure test
├── 4. Prepare New Zone
│   ├── Clean out wellbore
│   ├── Evaluate casing/cement
│   ├── Perforate new interval
│   └── Clean perforations
├── 5. Stimulate (if needed)
│   ├── Acidize or fracture
│   ├── Based on formation type
│   └── Optimize for zone
├── 6. Run New Completion
│   ├── Appropriate for zone
│   ├── Artificial lift if needed
│   ├── Set packer
│   └── Test all equipment
└── 7. Return to Production
    ├── Unload well
    ├── Test production
    ├── Optimize
    └── Monitor
```

## Adding Perforations

### Add-Perf Procedure

```
ADDING PERFORATIONS
├── Evaluation
│   ├── Why additional perfs?
│   ├── Where to perforate?
│   ├── How many shots/density?
│   └── Stimulation needed?
├── Wellbore Prep
│   ├── May need to kill well
│   ├── Or through-tubing perforating
│   ├── Clean wellbore
│   └── Verify depth correlation
├── Perforate
│   ├── Run perforating guns
│   ├── Correlate to depth
│   ├── Fire guns
│   ├── POOH with guns
│   └── Verify detonation
├── Post-Perf
│   ├── Flow well or test
│   ├── Stimulate if needed
│   ├── Evaluate contribution
│   └── Optimize production
└── Options
    ├── Through-tubing guns (live well)
    ├── Casing guns (higher performance)
    └── Coiled tubing conveyed
```

### Through-Tubing vs Casing Perforating

| Method | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Through-tubing | No completion pull, live well | Smaller guns, less penetration |
| Casing | Larger guns, better penetration | Must pull completion |
| CT conveyed | Precise depth, underbalanced | Equipment cost |

## Restimulation

### When to Restimulate

```
RESTIMULATION CANDIDATES
├── Production Decline
│   ├── Rate below expectations
│   ├── Skin damage suspected
│   ├── Pressure test indicates damage
│   └── Comparison to offsets poor
├── Formation Damage
│   ├── Scale buildup
│   ├── Paraffin/asphaltene
│   ├── Fines migration
│   ├── Water block
│   └── Bacterial plugging
├── Initial Stim Insufficient
│   ├── Understimulated
│   ├── Poor proppant placement
│   ├── Limited fracture length
│   └── Design improvements available
└── Economic Justification
    ├── Cost vs benefit analysis
    ├── Reserve recovery improvement
    └── Production increase forecast
```

### Restimulation Options

| Method | Application | Cost Level |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| Matrix acid | Carbonate damage removal | Low |
| Acid fracture | Carbonate stimulation | Medium |
| Hydraulic frac | Sandstone, shale | High |
| Refrac | Unconventional, depleted | High |
| Chemical treatment | Specific damage type | Low-Medium |

### Refrac Considerations

```
REFRAC PLANNING
├── Candidate Selection
│   ├── Original frac suboptimal
│   ├── Current technology better
│   ├── Remaining reserves justify cost
│   └── Mechanical integrity OK
├── Technical Approach
│   ├── Divert from existing fracs
│   ├── Access new rock
│   ├── May use diverters
│   └── Larger treatment design
├── Risks
│   ├── Higher pressure (stress shadow)
│   ├── May not access new rock
│   ├── Communication issues
│   └── Mechanical problems
└── Expected Results
    ├── Typically 50-150% increase
    ├── Varies widely
    └── Economics must work
```

## Dual Completions

### Dual Completion Types

```
DUAL COMPLETION OPTIONS
├── Parallel String
│   ├── Two tubing strings
│   ├── Separate packers
│   ├── Each zone independent
│   └── Full control each zone
├── Concentric
│   ├── One inside the other
│   ├── Limited applications
│   ├── Size restricted
│   └── Less common
├── Selective
│   ├── Sliding sleeves
│   ├── Single string
│   ├── Select zone at surface
│   └── Cannot produce simultaneously
└── Commingled
    ├── Single string
    ├── Both zones open
    ├── No separation
    └── Simple but limited control
```

### Dual Completion Considerations

| Factor | Parallel Dual | Commingled |
|--------|---------------|------------|
| Zone control | Excellent | None |
| Well testing | Each zone separate | Combined only |
| Artificial lift | Complex | Simple |
| Cost | High | Low |
| Intervention | Difficult | Standard |
| Allocation | Accurate | Estimated |

## Artificial Lift Changes

### Lift Method Conversion

```
COMMON LIFT CONVERSIONS
├── Rod Pump to ESP
│   ├── Higher rate needed
│   ├── Remove rods/pump
│   ├── Run ESP system
│   └── Power supply needed
├── ESP to Rod Pump
│   ├── Lower rate, cost savings
│   ├── Pull ESP
│   ├── Run rods and pump
│   └── Simpler operation
├── Add Gas Lift
│   ├── Gas available
│   ├── Run gas lift mandrels
│   ├── Install surface compressor
│   └── Optimize valve spacing
├── Install Plunger
│   ├── Low liquid, high GOR
│   ├── Install plunger system
│   ├── Surface controller
│   └── Optimize cycles
└── Convert to Flowing
    ├── If pressure recovered
    ├── Remove lift equipment
    ├── Simpler completion
    └── Lower operating cost
```

## Recompletion Risks

### Common Recompletion Problems

| Problem | Impact | Prevention |
|---------|--------|------------|
| Unable to isolate old zone | Continued production/injection | Proper squeeze, verify |
| New zone underperforms | Poor economics | Better evaluation |
| Mechanical failure | Extended workover | Quality equipment |
| Formation damage | Reduced production | Proper fluids |
| Stuck equipment | Fishing, cost | Good practices |
