# Glossary of Drilling Terms

## A

**Abandonment** - The permanent plugging of a well that is no longer productive or useful.

**Accumulator** - A vessel containing pressurized hydraulic fluid for operating BOP equipment.

**Annular Preventer** - A BOP that uses a rubber element to seal around any size pipe or close on open hole.

**Annulus** - The space between the drill string and the wellbore or casing.

**API** - American Petroleum Institute; organization that sets industry standards.

**APD** - Application for Permit to Drill; regulatory approval to begin drilling.

## B

**Back-off** - To unscrew drill pipe or other tubulars downhole.

**Barite** - Barium sulfate (BaSO4); weighting material for drilling mud.

**Barrel (bbl)** - Standard unit of measure for oil; 42 US gallons.

**BHA** - Bottom Hole Assembly; the lower portion of the drill string including bit, collars, and stabilizers.

**Bit** - The cutting tool at the end of the drill string that breaks rock.

**Blowout** - An uncontrolled flow of formation fluids to surface or into another formation.

**BOP** - Blowout Preventer; equipment used to seal the wellbore in an emergency.

**Bottoms Up** - Circulating long enough for fluid at the bit to return to surface.

## C

**Casing** - Steel pipe placed in the wellbore and cemented in place.

**Casing Point** - The planned depth at which casing will be set.

**CBL** - Cement Bond Log; wireline log evaluating cement quality.

**Circulating** - Pumping drilling fluid through the drill string and up the annulus.

**Company Man** - Operator's representative on the rig; oversees operations.

**Conductor** - The first, large-diameter casing set at shallow depth.

**Connection** - The process of adding a new joint of pipe while drilling.

**Core** - A cylindrical sample of formation rock cut for analysis.

## D

**DDR** - Daily Drilling Report; daily operations summary.

**Derrickman** - Crew member who works on the monkey board during trips.

**Deviation** - The angle of the wellbore from vertical.

**Differential Pressure** - The difference between mud hydrostatic and formation pressure.

**Directional Drilling** - Deliberately deviating a wellbore from vertical.

**Displacement** - Volume of fluid required to displace another fluid in the wellbore.

**Dogleg** - A sharp change in wellbore direction or inclination.

**DLS** - Dogleg Severity; rate of direction change per 100 feet.

**Drill Collars** - Heavy-walled tubulars providing weight on bit.

**Driller** - Crew member operating the drilling controls.

**Drilling Break** - A sudden increase in rate of penetration.

**Drill String** - The complete assembly of drill pipe, collars, and BHA.

## E

**ECD** - Equivalent Circulating Density; effective mud weight while pumping.

**EMW** - Equivalent Mud Weight; formation pressure expressed as density.

**ESP** - Electric Submersible Pump; artificial lift method.

## F

**FIT** - Formation Integrity Test; test to verify casing shoe strength.

**Float** - Check valve in the drill string preventing backflow.

**Flow Check** - Stopping pumps and watching for flow to detect kicks.

**Formation** - A geological rock unit with consistent characteristics.

**Frac Gradient** - Pressure required to fracture the formation, expressed as EMW.

## G

**Gas Cut** - Drilling fluid contaminated with formation gas.

**Gel Strength** - Ability of drilling fluid to suspend cuttings when static.

**GOR** - Gas-Oil Ratio; volume of gas produced per barrel of oil.

**Gunk Plug** - Mixture of diesel and bentonite used for lost circulation.

## H

**H2S** - Hydrogen Sulfide; poisonous gas sometimes found in formations.

**Hook Load** - Weight supported by the drilling hook.

**Hydrostatic Pressure** - Pressure exerted by a column of fluid at rest.

## I

**IBOP** - Inside Blowout Preventer; check valve installed in drill string.

**ICP** - Initial Circulating Pressure; pressure to start well kill circulation.

**Inclination** - The angle of the wellbore from true vertical.

## J

**Jar** - Downhole tool that delivers impact force to free stuck pipe.

**Jet Sub** - Tool allowing higher velocity fluid jetting for hole cleaning.

**Joint** - A single length of pipe, typically 30-31 feet.

## K

**Kelly** - Square or hexagonal pipe transmitting rotation to the drill string.

**Kick** - Influx of formation fluid into the wellbore.

**Kill** - Process of regaining well control after a kick.

**KMW** - Kill Mud Weight; mud weight required to balance formation pressure.

## L

**LCM** - Lost Circulation Material; material pumped to stop fluid losses.

**Lead Cement** - Lower-density cement placed in the larger annulus section.

**Liner** - Casing that doesn't extend to surface.

**LOT** - Leak-Off Test; test to determine formation fracture pressure.

**Lost Circulation** - Loss of drilling fluid to the formation.

## M

**MAASP** - Maximum Allowable Annular Surface Pressure.

**MD** - Measured Depth; depth measured along the wellbore.

**MMS** - Minerals Management Service (now BSEE/BOEM).

**Monkey Board** - Platform on the derrick where the derrickman works.

**Motor** - Positive displacement motor; downhole motor powered by mud flow.

**Mud** - Drilling fluid circulated through the wellbore.

**Mud Weight** - Density of drilling fluid, typically in ppg.

**MWD** - Measurement While Drilling; real-time downhole measurements.

## N

**NPT** - Non-Productive Time; time not contributing to well objectives.

**Nozzle** - Jets in the drill bit directing fluid flow.

## O

**OBM** - Oil-Based Mud; drilling fluid with oil as the continuous phase.

**Overbalance** - Condition where mud hydrostatic exceeds formation pressure.

**Overpull** - Tension applied above string weight when pulling.

## P

**P&A** - Plug and Abandon; permanently plugging and leaving a well.

**Packer** - Device that seals the annular space.

**PDC** - Polycrystalline Diamond Compact; type of drill bit.

**Perforating** - Creating holes through casing to allow reservoir communication.

**Pig** - Device pumped through pipe for cleaning or separation.

**Pit** - Tank holding drilling fluid.

**Pore Pressure** - Pressure of fluids contained in rock pores.

**POOH** - Pull Out Of Hole; tripping pipe out of the well.

**ppg** - Pounds per gallon; unit for drilling fluid density.

**PV** - Plastic Viscosity; measure of drilling fluid viscosity.

## R

**Ream** - Enlarging or cleaning the wellbore with the bit.

**Reserve Pit** - Pit for storing drilling waste.

**RIH** - Run In Hole; tripping pipe into the well.

**Riser** - Pipe connecting subsea BOP to the rig.

**ROP** - Rate of Penetration; drilling speed in feet per hour.

**Roughneck** - General term for drill crew member.

**Rotary Table** - Equipment that rotates the drill string.

**Round Trip** - Complete cycle of tripping out and back in.

## S

**Safety Meeting** - Pre-job discussion of hazards and precautions.

**SCR** - Slow Circulating Rate; reduced pump rate for well control.

**Shale Shaker** - Equipment for removing cuttings from drilling fluid.

**SICP** - Shut-In Casing Pressure; annulus pressure when well is closed.

**SIDPP** - Shut-In Drill Pipe Pressure; drill pipe pressure when well is closed.

**Sidetrack** - Drilling a new wellbore from an existing one.

**Slip** - Wedge-shaped device for holding pipe in the rotary table.

**Slug** - Heavy mud pumped into drill string before trips.

**Spud** - To begin drilling a well.

**Squeeze** - Forcing cement or other material into perforations or formation.

**Stuck Pipe** - Drill string that cannot be moved freely.

**Surge** - Pressure increase when running pipe into the hole.

**Survey** - Measurement of wellbore position (inclination and azimuth).

**Swab** - Pressure decrease when pulling pipe from the hole.

## T

**Tail Cement** - Higher-density cement placed across critical zones.

**TD** - Total Depth; the final depth of the well.

**Toolpusher** - Drilling contractor's supervisor on location.

**Top Drive** - System for rotating drill string from top of derrick.

**Torque** - Rotational force applied to the drill string.

**Trip** - Process of removing or running pipe in the hole.

**TVD** - True Vertical Depth; vertical depth from surface.

## U

**Underbalance** - Condition where formation pressure exceeds mud hydrostatic.

**Undergauge** - Hole diameter smaller than bit size.

## V

**VDL** - Variable Density Log; cement evaluation log.

## W

**Wait & Weight** - Kill method using weighted mud from the start.

**WBM** - Water-Based Mud; drilling fluid with water as the continuous phase.

**WOB** - Weight on Bit; downward force applied to the bit.

**WOC** - Wait on Cement; time for cement to set.

**Workover** - Operations on a producing well to maintain or improve production.

## Y

**Yield Point** - Measure of drilling fluid's ability to suspend cuttings.

---

*Terms and definitions may vary by region and company. This glossary covers common usage.*
