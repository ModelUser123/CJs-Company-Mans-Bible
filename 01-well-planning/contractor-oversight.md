# Contractor Oversight

## Overview

Effective contractor management is essential for safe, efficient, and cost-effective drilling operations. The Company Man is responsible for coordinating all contractors while maintaining operational control.

---

## Common Drilling Contractors

### Rig Contractor
- **Scope**: Rig equipment and personnel
- **Key Deliverables**: Safe drilling operations, equipment maintenance
- **Critical Interface**: All other contractors

### Directional Drilling
- **Scope**: Directional tools, MWD/LWD, surveys
- **Key Deliverables**: Well path execution, survey data
- **Critical Interface**: Mud logger, rig contractor

### Mud Company
- **Scope**: Drilling fluid design and maintenance
- **Key Deliverables**: Hole stability, formation evaluation support
- **Critical Interface**: Solids control, cuttings disposal

### Cementing
- **Scope**: Cement design, mixing, pumping
- **Key Deliverables**: Zonal isolation, casing support
- **Critical Interface**: Casing crew, rig contractor

### Wireline/Logging
- **Scope**: Formation evaluation, perforation, setting plugs
- **Key Deliverables**: Log data, completion services
- **Critical Interface**: Mud company, completion contractor

### Casing/Tubing
- **Scope**: Running tubulars
- **Key Deliverables**: Safe installation, connection integrity
- **Critical Interface**: Rig contractor, cementing

### Mud Logging
- **Scope**: Geological monitoring, gas detection
- **Key Deliverables**: Formation tops, shows, drilling parameters
- **Critical Interface**: Mud company, directional drilling

## Pre-Spud Contractor Management

### Contractor Qualification Checklist

```
□ HSE pre-qualification complete
□ Insurance certificates current
□ Master Service Agreement (MSA) in place
□ Work order/contract executed
□ Technical competency verified
□ Equipment certifications current
□ Personnel qualifications verified
□ Emergency contacts documented
```

### Pre-Spud Meeting Requirements

**All Contractors Must Provide:**
- Key personnel names and contact info
- Equipment specifications
- Delivery/mobilization schedule
- HSE documentation
- Emergency procedures
- Communication protocols

**Topics to Cover:**
1. Well objectives and program overview
2. Roles and responsibilities
3. Communication procedures
4. Safety expectations and STOP authority
5. Emergency response plans
6. Change management process
7. Reporting requirements
8. Performance expectations

## Problem Resolution

### Issue Escalation Process

**Level 1 - Immediate Resolution**
- Discuss with contractor representative on location
- Document issue and resolution
- No formal escalation required

**Level 2 - Supervisor Resolution**
- Formal written notice to contractor
- Company supervisor notified
- Resolution timeline established

**Level 3 - Management Resolution**
- Written notice to contractor management
- Company management involved
- Formal corrective action required

**Level 4 - Contract Action**
- Formal contract notice
- Performance penalties considered
- Potential contract termination

### Common Issues and Responses

| Issue | Initial Response | Escalation Trigger |
|-------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Late delivery | Expedite, adjust schedule | Repeat occurrence |
| Equipment failure | Repair/replace, track NPT | Pattern of failures |
| Personnel issue | Coach, document | Safety violation |
| Data quality | Verify, correct | Repeated errors |
| Cost overrun | Review, justify | Unauthorized charges |

## Contractor Safety Management

### Safety Expectations

**All Contractors Must:**
1. Comply with operator safety program
2. Attend all safety meetings
3. Submit to drug/alcohol testing
4. Report all incidents immediately
5. Participate in safety observations
6. Exercise STOP work authority

### Contractor HSE Requirements

```
□ JSA/JHA for all tasks
□ Permits to work (hot work, confined space, etc.)
□ PPE compliance
□ Equipment inspection tags current
□ MSDS/SDS available
□ Emergency contact info posted
□ Toolbox talks documented
```

### Short Service Employee (SSE) Program

**SSE Identification:**
- Personnel with < 6 months service company experience
- Personnel new to well type or region
- Personnel returning from extended absence

**SSE Requirements:**
- Buddy system with experienced personnel
- Additional supervision
- Limited high-risk task assignment
- Documented competency progression

## Contract Administration

### Invoice Review Checklist

```
□ Services rendered as per contract
□ Rates match contract
□ Hours/units correct
□ Third-party charges authorized
□ Backup documentation complete
□ Approved on field ticket
□ Coded to correct AFE
```

### Change Order Process

1. Contractor submits written request
2. Company Man reviews technical merit
3. Cost impact evaluated
4. Authorization obtained (if within authority)
5. Change order issued
6. Work proceeds
7. Final reconciliation
