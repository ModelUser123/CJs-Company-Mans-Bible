# Underbalanced Drilling (UBD)

## UBD Fundamentals

### What is Underbalanced Drilling?

```
UBD DEFINITION
├── BHP < Formation Pressure
│   ├── Formation flows while drilling
│   ├── Continuous influx managed
│   ├── Controlled at surface
│   └── Never shut-in well (normally)
├── Primary Purpose
│   ├── Minimize formation damage
│   ├── Increase ROP
│   ├── Reduce lost circulation
│   ├── Enable evaluation while drilling
│   └── Improve production
├── Key Differences from MPD
│   ├── UBD: Intentional influx
│   ├── MPD: Avoid influx (balanced)
│   ├── UBD: Flow to surface always
│   └── MPD: Closed system, no flow
└── Applications
    ├── Depleted zones
    ├── Fractured reservoirs
    ├── Sensitive formations
    ├── Low pressure zones
    └── Production enhancement critical
```

### UBD Methods

| Method | Density Range | Application |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| Gasified liquid | 4-8 ppg effective | Most common |
| Foam | 2-6 ppg effective | Low pressure |
| Mist | 1-3 ppg effective | Very low pressure |
| Air/gas | <1 ppg effective | Hard rock, dry |
| Flow drilling | Liquid | Moderate underbalance |

## Well Control

### UBD Well Control Philosophy

```
UBD WELL CONTROL
├── Different Philosophy
│   ├── Well is ALWAYS flowing
│   ├── Normal is "underbalanced"
│   ├── "Kick" is uncontrolled increase
│   ├── Primary barrier = surface equipment
│   └── BOP is backup
├── Monitoring
│   ├── Continuous flow measurement
│   ├── GOR/WOR tracking
│   ├── Pressure trending
│   ├── Separator level
│   └── Any abnormal changes
├── Influx Increase Response
│   ├── Increase choke pressure
│   ├── Slow/stop nitrogen
│   ├── Increase liquid rate
│   ├── Work toward balance
│   └── If uncontrollable → kill
└── Kill Decision
    ├── Equipment failure
    ├── Uncontrolled influx
    ├── Emergency
    └── End of UBD operations
```

### Emergency Procedures

```
UBD EMERGENCY RESPONSE
├── Uncontrolled Flow Increase
│   ├── Alert crew
│   ├── Close choke (increase BP)
│   ├── Increase liquid/reduce gas
│   ├── If unable to control:
│   │   ├── Close rotating head
│   │   ├── Stop pumps
│   │   ├── Close BOP
│   │   └── Kill well
│   └── Conventional well control
├── Equipment Failure
│   ├── Separator: Divert flow
│   ├── Choke: Use backup
│   ├── Rotating head: Close BOP
│   ├── Pumps: Kill weight ready
│   └── Always have contingency
├── Fire
│   ├── ESD activation
│   ├── Close well in
│   ├── Emergency response
│   └── Trained personnel
└── H2S Event
    ├── Monitors in place
    ├── Immediate response
    ├── SCBA usage
    └── Evacuation if needed
```

## Summary Table

| Aspect | UBD | MPD | Conventional |
|--------|-----|-----|--------------|
| BHP vs PP | Lower | Equal | Higher |
| Formation flow | Continuous | None | None (ideally) |
| Damage | Minimal | Reduced | Higher risk |

| Cost | Highest | Higher | Baseline |
| Applications | Specific | Broader | Widest |
