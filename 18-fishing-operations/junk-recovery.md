## Junk Detection

### Indicators of Junk in Hole

```
JUNK INDICATORS
├── While Drilling
│   ├── Sudden torque increase
│   ├── Erratic drilling
│   ├── Metal in returns
│   ├── Unusual vibration
│   └── Bit damage on retrieval
├── While Tripping
│   ├── Tag depth shallower than expected
│   ├── Weight indication of obstruction
│   ├── Unable to reach bottom
│   └── Resistance to rotation
├── On Wireline
│   ├── Cannot reach TD
│   ├── Tool hangs up
│   └── Unusual weight/tension
└── At Surface
    ├── Metal on shakers
    ├── Damaged returns
    ├── Missing components
    └── Tool inventory mismatch
```

### Junk Location Methods

| Method | Application | Accuracy |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Tag with string | Approximate depth | ±10 ft |
| Caliper log | Size/shape of obstruction | Good |
| Camera (downhole video) | Visual ID | Excellent |
| Impression block | Shape of top | Good |
| Calculated (from loss) | Known component | Variable |

## Junk Recovery Tools

### Fishing Magnets

```
MAGNET SPECIFICATIONS
├── Types
│   ├── Standard magnet
│   │   ├── Solid permanent magnet
│   │   ├── Maximum lift capacity
│   │   └── No circulation through
│   ├── Circulating magnet
│   │   ├── Central flow path
│   │   ├── Flushes debris toward face
│   │   └── Lower lift capacity
│   └── Boot sub magnet
│       ├── Used with junk basket
│       ├── Attracts and retains
│       └── Combination recovery
├── Sizing
│   ├── Face diameter vs hole size
│   ├── Need clearance for circulation
│   └── Larger = more capacity
├── Lift Capacity
│   ├── Rated in pounds
│   ├── Decreases with temperature
│   ├── Gap reduces force significantly
│   └── Typical: 50-500 lb
└── Limitations
    ├── Only ferrous metals
    ├── Gap critical
    ├── Embedded junk difficult
    └── Large pieces may not lift
```

### Junk Baskets

```
JUNK BASKET TYPES
├── Core-Type (Boot) Basket
│   ├── Fingers catch debris
│   ├── Circulation assists
│   ├── Run above bit/mill
│   ├── Best for small debris
│   └── Limited capacity
├── Reverse Circulation Basket
│   ├── Flow reverses in tool
│   ├── Debris lifted into basket
│   ├── Larger capacity
│   ├── Need special circulation
│   └── Best for finer debris
├── Poor Boy Basket
│   ├── Simple finger design
│   ├── Inexpensive
│   ├── Limited retention
│   └── Light duty only
└── Hydrostatic Basket
    ├── Uses pressure differential
    ├── Positive retention
    ├── Ball activates seal
    └── Excellent for all junk
```

### Junk Mills

```
JUNK MILLING APPROACH
├── When to Mill
│   ├── Junk cannot be fished
│   ├── Junk preventing operations
│   ├── Debris too small/embedded
│   └── Blocking bit passage
├── Mill Selection
│   ├── Flat bottom - general purpose
│   ├── Concave - round objects
│   ├── Pilot mill - prevents wallowing
│   └── Match to hole/junk size
├── Milling Parameters
│   ├── WOM: 2-8,000 lb (light)
│   ├── RPM: 60-120
│   ├── Flow: High for cleaning
│   └── Progress: Slow and steady
└── Recovery After Milling
    ├── Magnet for ferrous
    ├── Junk basket for debris
    ├── Multiple passes
    └── Verify clean before continuing
```

## Junk Recovery Procedures

### Standard Junk Recovery Run

```
JUNK FISHING PROCEDURE
├── Preparation
│   ├── Identify junk type and size
│   ├── Select appropriate tool
│   │   ├── Magnetic → Magnet
│   │   ├── Small debris → Junk basket
│   │   └── Unknown → Both
│   ├── Configure BHA
│   └── Brief crew
├── Running In
│   ├── Normal trip to above junk
│   ├── Slow down near obstruction
│   ├── Circulate clean
│   └── Note tag depth
├── Recovery Attempt
│   ├── Work tool over junk area
│   ├── Rotate slowly if applicable
│   ├── Circulate to assist
│   ├── Feel for loading
│   └── Pull slowly
├── Retrieval Check
│   ├── Check tool at surface
│   ├── Inventory recovered junk
│   ├── Compare to expected
│   └── Document findings
└── Repeat if Needed
    ├── Multiple runs common
    ├── May need different tool
    ├── Verify hole clean
    └── Proceed when satisfied
```

### Bit Cone Recovery

```
CONE RECOVERY PROCEDURE
├── Assessment
│   ├── How many cones lost?
│   ├── Estimated sizes
│   ├── Depth/position
│   └── Inserts also lost?
├── First Run - Magnet
│   ├── Strong fishing magnet
│   ├── Tag bottom carefully
│   ├── Work across bottom
│   ├── May get cone on first pass
│   └── Check returns for inserts
├── If Cone Not Recovered
│   ├── Run junk basket + magnet
│   ├── Mill on top of cone
│   ├── Work debris into basket
│   └── May need multiple passes
├── Insert Recovery
│   ├── Very small, difficult
│   ├── Reverse circulation helps
│   ├── Magnet for carbide
│   ├── Often milled and carried
│   └── Check shakers carefully
└── Verification
    ├── Run bit to bottom
    ├── Drill ahead carefully
    ├── Monitor for metal returns
    └── Proceed if clean
```

### Hand Tool Recovery

```
DROPPED HAND TOOL PROCEDURE
├── Immediate Actions
│   ├── Stop operations
│   ├── Identify what was dropped
│   ├── Note last known depth
│   └── Secure wellbore
├── Assessment
│   ├── Size and weight of tool
│   ├── Magnetic properties
│   ├── Position (on bit, in hole)
│   └── Obstruction to operations?
├── Recovery
│   ├── Usually magnetic → Magnet
│   ├── Tag carefully (don't push deeper)
│   ├── Work magnet over object
│   ├── Feel for loading
│   └── POOH and check
├── If Not Recovered
│   ├── Try junk basket
│   ├── May need to mill
│   ├── Small items may pass
│   └── Evaluate risk of leaving
└── Prevention
    ├── Secure all tools
    ├── Tethered equipment policy
    ├── Inventory before/after
    └── Enforce rig floor discipline
```

## Difficult Junk Situations

### Embedded Junk

```
EMBEDDED JUNK HANDLING
├── Situation
│   ├── Junk pressed into formation
│   ├── Cannot lift with magnet
│   ├── Below bit or tool level
│   └── May be in rathole
├── Options
│   ├── Washover to expose
│   │   ├── Mill around embedded area
│   │   ├── Free junk from formation
│   │   └── Recover with conventional tools
│   ├── Mill and bypass
│   │   ├── Mill into formation
│   │   ├── Destroy junk in place
│   │   └── Continue drilling
│   ├── Cement over
│   │   ├── Set plug above junk
│   │   ├── Drill out cement
│   │   ├── Leave junk entombed
│   │   └── Works for rathole junk
│   └── Leave in place
│       ├── If below TD
│       ├── If won't affect completion
│       └── Document thoroughly
```

MULTIPLE JUNK PIECES

*Junk recovery is often straightforward but can become very expensive if not handled properly. Prevention is always cheaper than recovery.*
