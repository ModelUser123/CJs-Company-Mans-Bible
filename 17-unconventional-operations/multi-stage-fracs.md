# Multi-Stage Hydraulic Fracturing

## Completion System Overview

### Plug-and-Perf System

```
PLUG-AND-PERF PROCESS
├── Pump down plug and perforating guns
├── Set plug at target depth
├── Correlate to target interval
├── Fire perforating guns
├── Retrieve guns to surface
├── Pump frac treatment
├── Repeat for each stage
└── Mill out plugs after completion

COMPONENTS
├── Composite Frac Plug
│   ├── Dissolvable or drillable
│   ├── Setting mechanism
│   ├── Slip system
│   └── Sealing element
├── Perforating Guns
│   ├── Shaped charges
│   ├── Gun diameter: 2-4"
│   ├── Shot density: 4-12 spf
│   └── Phasing: 60° or 90°
└── Pump-down Assembly
    ├── Tractor (if needed)
    ├── CCL (casing collar locator)
    └── Gamma ray (optional)
```

### Sliding Sleeve System

```
SLIDING SLEEVE COMPLETION
├── Run completion with sleeves
├── All sleeves initially closed
├── Open sleeves sequentially
│   ├── Ball-activated
│   ├── Coil tubing shifted
│   └── Intervention shifted
├── Pump frac through open sleeve
├── Close sleeve (if required)
└── Move to next stage

SLEEVE TYPES
├── Ball-Activated
│   ├── Size-sequenced balls
│   ├── Smallest to largest
│   ├── Typically 20-40 stages
│   └── Balls dissolve or mill
├── Coil-Shifted
│   ├── Mechanical shifting tool
│   ├── Unlimited stages
│   └── Requires CT intervention
└── Hybrid Systems
    ├── Combination of methods
    └── Optimized for application
```

### Comparison of Systems

| Feature | Plug-and-Perf | Sliding Sleeve |
|---------|--------------|----------------|
| Stage count | Unlimited | Limited by ball sizes |
| Cluster spacing | Flexible | Fixed at installation |
| Perforation control | Excellent | Pre-determined |
| Time per stage | 2-4 hours | 30 min - 1 hour |
| Intervention required | Yes (wireline) | No (ball drop) |
| Cost per stage | Higher | Lower |
| Flexibility | High | Low |
| Cleanout required | Yes (plug mill) | Minimal |

### Limited Entry Design

```
LIMITED ENTRY CONCEPT
├── Purpose: Even fluid distribution
├── Method: Restrict perforation count
├── Create pressure drop across perfs
└── Force fluid to all clusters

DESIGN EQUATIONS
├── Perforation Friction
│   Δp = 0.2369 × (Q²ρ) / (n² × D⁴ × Cd²)
│   Where:
│   ├── Δp = pressure drop (psi)
│   ├── Q = rate (bpm)
│   ├── ρ = fluid density (ppg)
│   ├── n = number of perfs
│   ├── D = perf diameter (in)
│   └── Cd = discharge coefficient
│
├── Target Δp: 500-1500 psi
│   ├── Higher Δp = better distribution
│   ├── Lower Δp = erosion concern
│   └── Balance is key
│
└── Rate per Perforation
    ├── Typical: 0.5-2.0 bpm/perf
    ├── Sand erosion limit: ~2 bpm
    └── Monitor erosion during job
```

## Frac Fluid Systems

### Slickwater

```
SLICKWATER CHARACTERISTICS
├── Base: Fresh or produced water
├── Friction Reducer: 0.5-2.0 gpt
├── Viscosity: 1-5 cp
├── Purpose
│   ├── Create complex fractures
│   ├── Maximize SRV
│   └── Low cost
├── Proppant Transport
│   ├── Limited (settling)
│   ├── High rate required
│   └── Fine mesh preferred
└── Typical Rates: 80-120 bpm

APPLICATIONS
├── Shale formations
├── Naturally fractured reservoirs
├── Low permeability targets
└── Water-sensitive formations (limited)
```

### Hybrid Fluids

```
HYBRID TREATMENT DESIGN
├── Slickwater Stages (Pad)
│   ├── Initiate fracture
│   ├── Create complexity
│   └── 30-40% of total volume
├── Crosslinked Stages
│   ├── Transport proppant
│   ├── Prop open fractures
│   └── 40-50% of total volume
└── Flush
    ├── Displace proppant
    └── 10-20% of volume

CROSSLINKED SYSTEMS
├── Borate
│   ├── Temperature: <250°F
│   ├── pH sensitive
│   └── Good clean break
├── Zirconate
│   ├── Temperature: 250-350°F
│   ├── Delayed crosslink
│   └── High temperature stability
└── Titanate
    ├── Temperature: 200-300°F
    ├── Good proppant transport
    └── Moderate stability
```

### Pressure Monitoring

```
CRITICAL PRESSURES
├── Surface Treating Pressure
│   ├── Monitor continuously
│   ├── Compare to design
│   └── Watch for trends
├── Instantaneous Shut-In Pressure (ISIP)
│   ├── Pressure when pumps stop
│   ├── Indicates near-wellbore friction
│   └── Monitor stage-to-stage
├── Bottomhole Treating Pressure (BHTP)
│   ├── BHTP = Surface + Hydrostatic - Friction
│   └── Must exceed frac gradient
└── Closure Pressure
    ├── DFIT determined
    └── Design consideration

PRESSURE TRENDING
├── ISIP Increasing
│   ├── May indicate stress shadow
│   ├── Possible communication
│   └── Consider spacing changes
├── ISIP Decreasing
│   ├── Possible fault/fracture hit
│   └── Communication with another well
└── Treating Pressure Spike
    ├── Screenout risk
    ├── Ball seat issue
    └── Reduce rate immediately
```


### Stage Pumping Sequence

```
PLUG-AND-PERF SEQUENCE
├── 1. Run Wireline
│   ├── Rig up lubricator
│   ├── Run plug/gun assembly
│   └── Pump down at 2-4 bpm
├── 2. Set Plug
│   ├── Correlate to depth
│   ├── Activate setting tool
│   └── Confirm set
├── 3. Perforate
│   ├── Position guns
│   ├── Correlate and fire
│   └── Confirm detonation
├── 4. Retrieve Guns
│   ├── Pull out of hole
│   └── Rig down lubricator
├── 5. Pump Treatment
│   ├── Pressure test lines
│   ├── Ball seat (if applicable)
│   ├── Pump design volumes
│   └── Monitor pressures
└── 6. Move to Next Stage
    ├── Prep next plug/gun
    └── Repeat process
```

### Post-Stage Analysis

| Parameter | Target | Action if Out of Range |
|-----------|--------|----------------------|
| ISIP | Within 200 psi of design | Evaluate stress model |
| Proppant placed | 100% of design | Adjust future stages |
| Treating pressure | Below limit | Continue as planned |
| Screenout | None | Adjust loading/rate |
| Stage time | On schedule | Optimize operations |

## Screenout Management

### Screenout Indicators

```
WARNING SIGNS
├── Pressure Increase
│   ├── Gradual rise during proppant
│   ├── Steepening trend
│   └── Approaching limits
├── Rate Response
│   ├── Decreasing rate at constant pressure
│   └── Pump efficiency dropping
├── Proppant Concentration
│   ├── High concentration in late stage
│   └── Approaching sand-out
└── Historical Performance
    ├── Previous stages had issues
    └── Formation tendency

SCREENOUT RESPONSE
├── Immediate Actions
│   ├── Reduce proppant concentration
│   ├── Increase rate (if possible)
│   ├── Add friction reducer
│   └── Consider flush
├── If Pressure Continues Rising
│   ├── Flush immediately
│   ├── Record final ISIP
│   └── Document proppant placed
└── Post-Screenout
    ├── Evaluate cause
    ├── Adjust future stage design
    └── Consider remedial action
```

### Prevention Strategies

| Risk Factor | Prevention |
|-------------|------------|
| High near-wellbore friction | Extended pad, higher rate |
| Poor proppant transport | Better fluid design |
| Narrow fracture | Increase pad volume |
| High proppant loading | Reduce concentration |
| Complex fractures | Increase rate, lower conc |

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues and Solutions

| Issue | Possible Cause | Solution |
|-------|---------------|----------|
| Can't break down | Plugged perfs, wrong depth | Re-perf, verify depth |
| High treating pressure | Near-wellbore tortuosity | Increase rate, add acid |
| Premature screenout | Formation, design | Reduce loading, more pad |
| Communication | Stage too close | Skip stage, continue |
| Lost ball | Wrong size, damage | Retrieve, replace |
| Pump failures | Mechanical | Use redundancy |
| Wireline issues | Line damage, tool fail | Re-run, replace |
